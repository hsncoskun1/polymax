## Investigation Result

**No bug found.** The implementation was already correct.

`market_discovery.py` line 137:
```python
duration = (market.end_date - market.source_timestamp).total_seconds()
```

This is `end_date − source_timestamp` — **total structural duration**.
There is no `datetime.now()`, `utcnow()`, or any reference to current time anywhere in `DiscoveryService` or `PolymarketFetchService`.

**However:** this contract was not test-locked. A future change could silently introduce remaining-time logic without a failing test. This version adds the tests that prevent that.

---

## The Two Calculations — Why It Matters

| Approach | Formula | Meaning | Discovery's Job? |
|----------|---------|---------|-----------------|
| **Total duration** (correct) | `end_date − source_timestamp` | Is this a 5m structured market? | ✅ Yes |
| **Remaining time** (wrong for discovery) | `end_date − now` | Is it too late to enter? | ❌ No — runtime/strategy layer |

A near-expiry valid market:
- started 4 minutes ago, ends in 1 minute
- total duration = 300 s → valid [240, 360]
- remaining time = 60 s → would be invalid if remaining-time based

Discovery must accept it. "Entry timing" is a separate layer's responsibility.

## Contract Now Locked

> **DiscoveryService evaluates `end_date − source_timestamp` (total structural duration).
> It does not evaluate `end_date − now` (remaining time).
> A near-expiry market with valid total duration must always be a candidate.**

Any change to `market_discovery.py` that introduces `datetime.now()` or remaining-time logic will now cause multiple tests to fail immediately.
